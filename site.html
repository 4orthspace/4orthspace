The Idea

We Believe There's
A Better Way.

Traditional social responsibility often focuses on what is given.

We focus on what people gain.

Because lasting change isn't created through a single moment of generosity. It's created through experiences that build resilience, encourage participation, create opportunities, and bring people together. That's why every programme we design is built around people, not just projects.

Sociologists identified three spaces that hold a life together. We built the fourth.

First space

Home

Where you rest. Comfortable, but rarely where momentum is found.

Second space

Work

Where you're paid. Structured, scheduled, and shaped by someone else.

Third space

The social

Cafés, clubs, lounges. Welcome, but designed for everyone at once.

Fourth space — 4orth

Here

Curated, purposeful, and built for impact. Where ambition and social responsibility are finally at home.

What Is An Impact Experience?

More than an event.
More than a CSR initiative.

It's a carefully designed programme that helps people feel more connected, more confident, and more capable.

We combine global ideas with local insight to create experiences that are culturally relevant, engaging, and designed to leave communities stronger than we found them.

What Makes 4orth Space Different

We don't start by asking,
"What should we donate?"

"What experience could genuinely improve people's lives?"

That thinking has led us to develop programmes inspired by global cultural movements — from racket sports that build community and wellbeing to self-defence workshops that develop confidence and resilience.

Every experience is designed to create meaningful participation while helping organisations build authentic relationships with the communities they serve.

From the ground up

The journey

Every wall, every detail, every choice — documented. This is how 4orth went from an idea to a place you can walk into today.

The beginning
The idea takes shape
It started with a question: what would a space built around purpose — not productivity — actually look like? We found the building in Guzape and knew immediately.
Demolition & groundwork
Stripping it back to build it right
Before anything could go in, everything had to come out. Walls came down, floors were stripped, and we started to see the bones of what 4orth could become.
Construction
Taking form
Structural work, electrical fit-out, plumbing, acoustic treatment. The kind of behind-the-scenes decisions that make the difference between a space that works and one that doesn't.
Fit-out & finishing
The details that make a place
Furniture arrived, walls were painted in the palette we'd spent weeks debating, the logo went up. The space started to feel like 4orth.
During construction
Guests on site
Even before the paint dried, people were coming to see what was taking shape. Early supporters, curious neighbours, collaborators, and friends — all of them part of the story before it officially began.
Opening
The doors open
The first people walked through the doors and the space came alive. Everything we'd built it for — people gathering, connecting, creating — was finally happening.

Our Services

We Don't Just Deliver Programmes.
We Design Impact Experiences.

Every organisation has a purpose. Our job is to bring that purpose to life through experiences that strengthen communities, inspire participation, and create meaningful human outcomes.

From strategy to delivery, 4orth Space manages every stage of the journey.

01

Impact Experience Design

Designing programmes that align with your objectives and the needs of the communities you want to reach.

02

Community Engagement

Creating authentic opportunities to engage people through sport, culture, education, and wellbeing.

03

Programme Delivery

From concept to execution — seamless, responsible delivery with genuine community relevance.

04

Strategic Partnerships

Connecting organisations with trusted community leaders, facilitators, and specialist partners.

05

Measurement & Legacy

Understanding what changed and how to build on that momentum through meaningful evaluation.
